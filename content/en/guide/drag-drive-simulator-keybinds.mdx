The default keyboard configuration maps essential driving mechanics to the left side of the keyboard. This allows you to maintain steering control with your left hand while utilizing your mouse to adjust camera angles or navigate menus.

| Action | Keybind | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Throttle / Accelerate | W / Up Arrow | Increases engine RPM and drives the vehicle forward |
| Brake / Reverse | S / Down Arrow | Slows down the vehicle or reverses when fully stopped |
| Steer Left | A / Left Arrow | Turns the front wheels or leans your motorcycle left |
| Steer Right | D / Right Arrow | Turns the front wheels or leans your motorcycle right |
| Shift Up (Gear +) | E | Upshifts to the next gear in manual transmission mode |
| Shift Down (Gear -) | Q | Downshifts to a lower gear in manual transmission mode |
| Clutch | Left Shift | Disengages the engine from the transmission for revving |
| Handbrake | Spacebar | Locks the rear wheels for staging or drift initiation |
| Nitrous Oxide (NOS) | F | Activates your equipped power boost system |
| Change Camera View | V | Toggles between first-person and third-person perspectives |

<Callout type="warning" title="Anti-Ghosting Hardware Warning">
Make sure your keyboard supports multi-key rollover. Holding down W, Left Shift, and pressing E simultaneously can cause input failure on older membrane keyboards due to hardware ghosting.

Simply memorizing the default layout is only the first step. To dominate the drag strip, you must master the physics of launching and shifting. Manual transmission offers significantly higher top speeds and faster acceleration compared to automatic mode, but it requires precise execution of key inputs.

| Vehicle Class | Optimal Launch RPM | Perfect Shift Window | Acceleration Rating |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sport Motorcycles | 8,000 RPM | 11,000 - 11,500 RPM | Excellent (5/5) |

When staging, your tires need to heat up. Holding the handbrake and spinning the tires increases tire temperature, which directly improves the traction coefficient. If you launch with cold tires, you will experience excessive wheelspin, wasting precious milliseconds at the starting line.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Stage Your Vehicle">
    Line up at the starting line and hold the Handbrake (Spacebar) to keep your vehicle completely stationary.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Build Engine RPM">
    Hold down the Throttle (W) while keeping the Handbrake engaged to rev your engine to the optimal launch RPM.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Release and Launch">
    Release the Handbrake exactly when the light turns green while keeping the Throttle fully depressed.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute Perfect Shifts">
    Watch your tachometer closely and press the Shift Up key (E) right as the needle enters the green zone.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Shifting Tip">
Shifting too early causes the engine to bog down below its power band, while shifting too late bounces the engine off the rev limiter. Both mistakes severely hurt your quarter-mile times.
</Callout>

For players who prefer a more ergonomic racing experience, the game supports full controller integration and customizable mobile layouts. Utilizing a gamepad offers analog steering precision, which is highly beneficial when handling high-horsepower rear-wheel-drive cars that are prone to sliding.

Mobile controls can be tricky due to the lack of tactile feedback. It is highly recommended to use the on-screen static buttons rather than tilt steering for drag racing. Tilt steering introduces input latency that can ruin your launch timing and cause you to drift out of your lane.
