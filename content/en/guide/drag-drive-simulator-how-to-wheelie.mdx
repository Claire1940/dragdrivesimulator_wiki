<Section icon={Target} title="The Ultimate Wheelie Tuning Setup">

The secret to **drag drive simulator how to wheelie** lies within the Tuning Menu. You cannot expect a stock vehicle to lift its front end consistently. You must adjust the mechanical balance of your ride to favor explosive acceleration over top-end speed.

### Gear Ratio Optimization
To get that front wheel up, you need a "Short" gear setup. Shorter ratios provide more torque at low speeds, which is the primary driver for wheelies.

| Gear Stage | Recommended Setting | Impact on Wheelie |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1st Gear** | **Very Short** | Maximum lift potential during launch |
| **2nd Gear** | Short | Maintains wheelie through the first shift |
| **3rd Gear** | Medium | Transitions into high-speed stability |
| **Final Drive** | High | Balances top speed for the finish line |

<Callout type="warning" title="Tuning Risk">
Setting your gears too short can cause the engine to redline instantly, resulting in a "loop" where the bike flips backward. Always test your 1st gear ratio at **Sentul Track** before entering a competitive race.
</Callout>

### Suspension Pro Settings
If you have the **Suspension Pro Pass** (29 Robux), you gain access to damping and stiffness controls. This is the "secret sauce" for professional wheelie builds. By softening the rear suspension and stiffening the front, you encourage the weight to transfer to the rear during acceleration.

| Component | Setting | Wheelie Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Rear Stiffness** | **Soft** | Allows rear to squat, pushing front up |
| **Front Stiffness** | Stiff | Prevents front-end bounce on landing |
| **Rear Damping** | High | Controls the rate of weight transfer |
| **Ride Height** | Elevated | Raises center of gravity for easier lift |

  <Step num="2" title="The Power Launch">

  <Step num="3" title="Balance Point Management">

  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Shifting for Longevity">
    To keep the wheelie going through higher speeds, shift into 2nd gear just before the engine hits the rev limiter. The sudden surge of torque in the next gear will help keep the front end elevated.
  </Step>
</Steps>
