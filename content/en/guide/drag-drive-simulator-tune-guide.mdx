<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering Gear Ratios for Drag Racing">

The most critical component of the **drag drive simulator tune guide** is the gear ratio system. In the tuning menu, you will see sliders for each gear (usually 1 through 6) and a Final Drive ratio. Adjusting these determines how quickly your vehicle reaches its redline and how much top speed it can ultimately achieve.

| Ratio Type | Acceleration Impact | Top Speed Impact | Recommended Race Type |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Shorter Gears** | Massive Increase | Significant Decrease | 200m / 400m Drag Strips |
| **Longer Gears** | Noticeable Decrease | Massive Increase | Highway / Top Speed Runs |
| **Balanced Setup** | Moderate | Moderate | Circuit Racing / Daily Driving |
| **Aggressive Launch** | Extreme (Gear 1-2) | N/A | Competitive Drag Tournament |

When setting up for a drag race, your goal is to have Gear 1 and Gear 2 be short enough to get you off the line instantly without excessive wheelspin. As you progress into higher gears, you should gradually lengthen the ratios to ensure the engine stays within its peak power band until you cross the finish line.

<Callout type="warning" title="Tuning Trap">
Avoid setting your Final Drive too high. While it improves acceleration across all gears, it can cause your vehicle to "bounce" off the rev limiter before you reach the end of the track, wasting precious seconds.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Step-by-Step Tuning Process">

Follow these steps to create a competitive race setup from scratch. This process works for both motorcycles and cars, though motorcycles generally require more precise adjustments due to their higher RPM limits.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Baseline Run">
    Take your vehicle to Sentul Track in its stock configuration. Record your 400m time and your top speed at the finish line. This provides the data needed to see if your tunes are actually improving performance.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Adjust the Final Drive">
    Start by shortening the Final Drive ratio slightly. This provides a universal boost to acceleration. If your vehicle hits its top speed too early, lengthen it back out.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Optimize Lower Gears">
    Adjust Gear 1 and Gear 2 so that you launch at the highest possible RPM without losing traction. You want the shift from 1st to 2nd to happen quickly but smoothly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Stretch the Top End">
    Set Gear 5 and Gear 6 to be longer. Ideally, you should reach the redline in your final gear exactly as you cross the finish line of your preferred track.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Validation and Testing">
    Perform three consecutive runs. If your times are consistent and faster than the baseline, save the tune. If the vehicle feels sluggish, revert the last change and try a different increment.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Pro Tip: Suspension Tuning">
If you have the **Suspension Pro Pass**, lower your ride height and increase stiffness for drag races. This reduces air resistance and prevents the front end from lifting during high-torque launches.
</Callout>

</Section>
