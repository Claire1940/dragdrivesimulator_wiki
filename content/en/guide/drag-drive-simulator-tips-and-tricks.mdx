<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Gear Ratio Tuning and Customization">

Tuning is the absolute core of vehicle performance in Drag Drive Simulator. A cheap, finely tuned motorcycle can easily outrun a stock luxury sports car on the drag strip [4]. Understanding how gear ratios interact with your vehicle's power band is crucial to winning races.

| Upgrade Type | Access Method | Cost (Robux) | Performance & Visual Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Gear Ratio Tuning** | Base Game System | Free | Adjusts acceleration and top speed limits |
| **Pro Suspension** | Suspension Pro Pass | 29 Robux | Provides advanced damping and stiffness control |
| **Advanced Paint** | Advance Paint Pass | 59 Robux | Unlocks HEX color support, carbon fiber, and matte finishes |
| **Custom Plates** | Custom Plate Pass | 79 Robux | Allows personalized text on vehicle license plates |

When adjusting your gear ratios, you must find a balance between acceleration and top speed:
- **Shorter Gears**: Dramatically improves off-the-line launch and acceleration. However, this caps your maximum speed much earlier.
- **Longer Gears**: Lowers initial launch power but allows the vehicle to reach much higher top-end speeds over longer distances.
- **Optimal Balance**: Adjust your gears so that you reach peak RPM in your final gear right as you cross the finish line.

Experimenting with small adjustments is the safest way to find the perfect tune for your specific vehicle.

<Callout type="warning" title="Tuning Warning">
Avoid extreme gear ratio adjustments. Pulling your gears too short will cause your vehicle to redline instantly and lose acceleration, while setting them too long will make your launch incredibly sluggish.
</Callout>
