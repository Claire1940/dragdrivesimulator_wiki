<Section icon={Settings} title="Tuning for ROI: Gear Ratios Explained">

You cannot separate money-making from tuning in **drag drive simulator**. A vehicle that cannot win races is a liability, not an asset. The Gear Ratio system is the most important mechanic to master if you want to turn your vehicle into a profit machine. Tuning allows you to sacrifice top speed for acceleration (Shorter Ratios) or vice versa (Longer Ratios).

For drag racing, you generally want **Shorter Ratios** in the first three gears to get off the line as fast as possible. However, if your ratios are too short, you will "redline" (hit maximum RPM) too early and lose speed in the final stretch of the track.

| Tuning Goal | Gear Ratio Adjustment | Result on Track |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Faster Launch** | Decrease Ratio (Short) | High acceleration, lower top speed |
| **Higher Top Speed** | Increase Ratio (Long) | Slower start, higher maximum velocity |
| **Balanced Build** | Mid-range settings | Good for road racing and cruising |
| **Drag Spec** | Aggressive Short Start | Optimized for 201m or 402m sprints |

<Callout type="tip" title="Incremental Changes">
When tuning, only change one gear ratio at a time by a small margin (e.g., 0.1 or 0.2). Test the launch in free roam before committing to a full race. This prevents you from "breaking" a setup and losing your competitive edge.
</Callout>

</Section>
