<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Tuning Process">

Tuning your transmission requires a systematic approach. Making random adjustments to multiple gears at once makes it impossible to identify which changes improved your quarter-mile times [4]. Follow this structured process to build a highly optimized custom tune [4].

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish Your Baseline Run">
    Before changing any values, take your vehicle to Sentul Track and perform three baseline runs [4]. Record your launch reaction time, 0-60 MPH split, and final trap speed to use as a comparison metric [4].
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Adjust the Final Drive Ratio">
    Open the customization menu and locate the final drive slider [4]. If you are consistently hitting your top speed before the finish line, lower the final drive (longer ratio) [4]. If you never reach your top gear, raise the final drive (shorter ratio) [4].
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Configure Individual Gear Steps">
    Adjust individual gear sliders to create a smooth progression [4]. First gear should be short for launch traction [4]. Subsequent gears should gradually decrease in numerical value, keeping the engine within its optimal RPM power band after each shift [4].
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Validate and Refine on the Track">
    Return to the track and perform test passes [4]. If the vehicle wheelspins excessively at launch, lengthen first gear slightly [4]. If the engine bogs down (drops below the power band) after a shift, shorten that specific gear [4].
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Over-Shortening">
Setting individual gears too short will cause the transmission to shift constantly, wasting valuable milliseconds during gear transitions and severely capping your top-end speed [4].
</Callout>
