### Understanding Gear Ratios

The gear ratio system is a delicate balancing act between acceleration and top speed. In Drag Drive Simulator, you have the freedom to adjust each gear's ratio individually. Shorter gear ratios provide rapid acceleration but cap your top speed early. Conversely, longer gear ratios take longer to build momentum but offer a much higher top-end velocity.

| Gear Setup | Acceleration Speed | Top-End Velocity | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Shorter Gears** | Rapid launch off the line | Lower maximum speed | First and second gears to prevent bogging down |
| **Longer Gears** | Slower initial build-up | Higher maximum speed | Fourth, fifth, and sixth gears to stretch top-end |

To maximize your quarter-mile performance, your vehicle must cross the finish line just as it hits its absolute maximum speed in its final gear. If you top out too early, you lose valuable seconds cruising at a speed limit. If your gears are too long, the race will end before you even reach your top gear, wasting your engine's potential.

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip">
Never adjust all your gears to the absolute minimum or maximum values. Smooth transitions between gears prevent your RPM from dropping drastically during shifts.
</Callout>

Achieving the perfect transmission setup requires patience and systematic testing. Instead of guessing values in the customization menu, follow this structured process to dial in your gear ratios. <Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish Your Baseline Run">
    Take your stock vehicle to a quiet, straight stretch of road. Record your launch time, shift points, and the top speed you achieve before running out of asphalt. This baseline serves as your point of comparison for all future adjustments.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Optimize the Launch Gears">
    Access the modification menu and shorten your first and second gears slightly. This adjustment increases torque and helps your vehicle launch harder off the starting line. Test the launch to ensure the tires do not spin excessively, which wastes traction.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Smooth the Mid-Range Transitions">
    Adjust third and fourth gears to create a smooth progression. You want your engine to stay within its optimal power band immediately after shifting. If the engine bogs down and loses RPM, shorten the receiving gear.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Stretch the Top-End Gears">
    Lengthen your fifth and sixth gears to maximize your top-end velocity. The goal is to reach your peak velocity right as you cross the finish line at Sentul Track. Adjust these final gears based on whether you are running short time trials or longer drag races.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Vehicle Tuning Profiles

Different vehicle categories require distinct tuning philosophies due to their unique weight, traction, and power characteristics. For example, motorcycles are incredibly lightweight and can launch with immense acceleration, but they often struggle to maintain stability at extreme top speeds compared to heavy-duty drag cars.

| Vehicle Category | Launch Trait | Gear Progression | Tuning Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Motorcycles** | Highly explosive off the line | Rapid early shifts | Short lower gears, progressive mid-range stretch |
| **Standard Cars** | Moderate initial traction | Steady RPM build-up | Balanced gear ratios to maintain power band |
| **Drag Spec** | Elite grip with wide rear tires | Ultra-precise shift windows | Aggressive launch gears with long top-end gears |
