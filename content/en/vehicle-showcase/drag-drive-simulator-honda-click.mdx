<Section icon={Settings} title="Tuning and Gear Ratio Optimization">

The heart of the **drag drive simulator honda click** experience is the tuning menu. If you leave your scooter with stock settings, you will likely lose to players with lower-tier bikes who have optimized their ratios. Tuning in this simulator revolves around the balance between "Short" and "Long" gears.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Baseline Test">
    Take your Honda Click to a flat stretch of road. Record your 0-100 km/h time and your maximum top speed. This serves as your "control" data.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Adjusting the Final Drive">
    For drag racing, you want a shorter final drive. This increases torque at the wheels, allowing for a more aggressive launch. If your bike wheelies too much, lengthen it slightly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Individual Gear Smoothing">
    Ensure that your RPM doesn't drop too significantly between shifts. A "tight" gear spread keeps the engine in its power band, which is crucial for the Honda Click's small-displacement engine.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Validation at Sentul">
    Run a full quarter-mile at Sentul Circuit. If you hit your top speed before the finish line, your gears are too short. If you are still accelerating past the finish, they are too long.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Tuning Setting | Effect on Performance | Recommended For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Shorter Ratios** | **Higher Acceleration** / Lower Top Speed | 201m and 402m Drag Races |
| **Longer Ratios** | Lower Acceleration / **Higher Top Speed** | Highway Cruising / Top Speed Runs |
| **Pro Suspension** | Better Stability / Reduced Bounce | High-speed stability on bumpy roads |
| **Drag Tires** | **Maximum Grip** / Reduced Wheelspin | Competitive Drag Strip events |

<Callout type="success" title="Tuning Secret">
The Honda Click benefits immensely from a "Longer" 5th and 6th gear. This allows the bike to continue pulling even when wind resistance increases at high speeds, preventing the engine from "bogging down."
</Callout>
