<Section icon={Gauge} title="Tuning Guide for Maximum Performance">

Owning the **drag drive simulator best bike** is only half the battle. Without a proper tune, even a ZX10R will lose to a well-adjusted budget bike. Tuning in 2026 focuses heavily on the gear ratio system, which dictates how your bike translates engine power into forward momentum.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Gear Calibration">
    Start by setting your first gear to a shorter ratio. This provides the explosive launch needed to gain an early lead in drag races.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Mid-Range Smoothing">
    Adjust gears two through four to ensure the RPM doesn't drop too low during shifts. You want to stay within the "power band" of the motorcycle.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Top-End Extension">
    Set your final gear to a longer ratio. This prevents the engine from redlining too early, allowing you to reach maximum velocity on long tracks like Pettarani.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Suspension Pro Adjustments">
    If you own the Suspension Pro Pass, stiffen the rear dampening. This reduces "squat" during the launch, keeping more power going directly to the pavement.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Track Type | Gear Ratio Focus | Recommended Bike | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Short Drag** | Short / Aggressive | RX KING | High torque, instant launch |
| **Long Circuit** | Balanced / Long | ZX25R | Maintain high RPM over time |
| **City Roaming** | Smooth / Wide | PCX 160 | Fuel efficiency and easy handling |

<Callout type="warning" title="Tuning Warning">
Avoid making massive jumps in gear ratios. Small increments of 0.05 to 0.10 are usually enough to see a significant difference in track times. Over-tuning can cause the engine to bog down or hit the rev limiter prematurely.
</Callout>
