<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Tuning the Vario for Maximum Performance">

Tuning is the heart of **drag drive simulator vario** gameplay. Unlike cars, scooters in this game rely heavily on maintaining high RPMs through a CVT-like simulation. If your gear ratios are too long, the Vario will "bog down" at the start. If they are too short, you will hit the rev limiter before reaching the finish line.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Gear Setup">
    Access the tuning menu in free roam. Set your **Final Drive** to a balanced mid-point. For the Vario, a shorter final drive (higher numerical value) helps with the initial "pop" off the line.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Ratio Refinement">
    Adjust Gear 1 and Gear 2 to be very close together. This simulates the rapid acceleration of a performance CVT system. You want to stay in the power band as long as possible.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Suspension & Damping">
    If you have the **Suspension Pro Pass**, lower the rear of the Vario. This reduces wheelie potential during high-torque launches, keeping your power on the asphalt.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Validation Run">
    Head to **Sentul Track**. Record your 402m time. If you hit the top speed before the 350m mark, lengthen your final gear slightly to gain more top-end speed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Gear Setting | Short Track (201m) | Long Track (402m) | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Final Drive** | 4.10 - 4.50 | 3.50 - 3.80 | Overall Speed vs Torque |
| **1st Gear** | 3.20 | 2.90 | Launch Grip |
| **2nd Gear** | 2.10 | 1.85 | Mid-Range Pull |
| **3rd Gear** | 1.50 | 1.30 | Top-End Extension |

<Callout type="success" title="Tuning Success">
A "perfect" Vario tune should see the needle staying in the red zone of the tachometer without bouncing off the limiter until the very end of the race.
</Callout>

</Section>
