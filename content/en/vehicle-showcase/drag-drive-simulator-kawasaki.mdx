<Section icon={Target} title="Gear Ratio Tuning Step-by-Step">

Tuning is the beating heart of performance in this simulation. You can have the most expensive superbike in the server, but with a poor gear ratio setup, you will constantly lose to cheaper, well-tuned machines. Tuning in this experience revolves around balancing shorter and longer gears. Shorter gears give you an explosive launch off the line but cap your top speed early. Longer gears allow for a massive top-end speed but suffer from sluggish acceleration.

For Kawasaki bikes, which thrive in high-RPM ranges, finding the sweet spot is essential. Follow these steps to optimize your motorcycle for drag racing:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Access the Customization Garage">
    Drive your Kawasaki into any local tuning shop or open your garage menu in free roam to access the performance tuning interface.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Adjust the Final Drive Ratio">
    Set your final drive ratio to a balanced midpoint. A higher final drive increases acceleration, while a lower final drive extends your top-end speed.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Fine-Tune Individual Gears">
    Shorten your 1st and 2nd gears to ensure a perfect, wheel-spin-free launch. Gradually lengthen 3rd through 6th gears to maintain acceleration throughout the run.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Perform Test Launches">
    Take your bike to the Sentul Track or Pettarani Track. Run multiple passes, noting down your 0-60 times and top speed at the finish line.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once you have mastered the basic gear ratios, you can begin experimenting with advanced setups. Highly specialized drag racers will often run incredibly short setups designed specifically for 201-meter or 402-meter tracks, while highway cruisers will opt for long-ratio setups that maximize top speed over long distances.

| Gear Profile | Acceleration Rate | Top Speed Potential | Recommended Track |
| **Short Drag Tune** | Extremely High | Moderate / Capped | Sentul Track (Short) |
| **Long Highway Tune** | Moderate / Slow Launch | Extremely High | Surakarta Highway |
| **Balanced Track Tune** | High | High | Pettarani Track (Mixed) |
