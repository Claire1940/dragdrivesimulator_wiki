<Section icon={Target} title="Tuning and Customization Guide">

Unlocking the full potential of your vehicle requires precise adjustments in the garage. Even an S-Tier car can perform poorly if the gear ratios are mismatched or the tire pressure is set incorrectly. Tuning allows you to adapt your vehicle to different track lengths and surface conditions.

To achieve optimal times, you must balance traction against engine resistance. Lowering tire pressure increases the contact patch for better grip but introduces rolling resistance at high speeds. Similarly, shorter gear ratios improve acceleration but limit your maximum top speed.

<Callout type="warning" title="Tuning Risks">
Avoid setting your launch control RPM too high on RWD vehicles. Doing so will cause excessive wheelspin, overheating the tires and severely increasing your quarter-mile times.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Optimize Tire Pressure">
    Access the garage menu and lower your rear tire pressure to **15-18 PSI** for RWD cars, or **18-20 PSI** on all four wheels for AWD platforms to maximize launch grip.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Adjust Gear Ratios">
    Shorten the first and second gears to keep the engine in its peak powerband immediately after launching. Lengthen the final drive ratio to prevent hitting the rev limiter before the finish line.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Stiffen the Suspension">
    Increase the rear spring stiffness to minimize chassis squat during acceleration. This keeps the vehicle level and ensures consistent power delivery to the drive wheels.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Configure Launch Control">
    Set your launch control RPM to match the peak torque RPM of your engine. Test the launch in practice mode and adjust in 100 RPM increments until wheelspin is minimized.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The following matrix explains how different tuning adjustments impact your vehicle's performance on the track. Use this as a reference when fine-tuning your setup.
