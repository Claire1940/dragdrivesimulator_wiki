<Section icon={Target} title="The Core Mechanics: Precision Tuning Guide">

Tuning is where the **drag drive simulator bilao concept** truly comes to life. The gear ratio system is the most critical component of performance. A vehicle with a higher price tag will often lose to a cheaper, well-tuned bike if the gear ratios are not optimized for the specific track length.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Base Performance Testing">
    Take your stock vehicle to the Sentul Track. Record your 0-100 km/h time and your top speed at the finish line. This provides the baseline for all future adjustments.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Initial Gear Adjustment">
    Access the Tuning menu. If your vehicle reaches its top speed long before the finish line, your gears are too "short." Increase the ratio for a "longer" gear to extend your top-end speed.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Launch Optimization">
    Adjust the first and second gears to prevent excessive wheelspin while maintaining enough torque to launch aggressively. This is the "sweet spot" for drag racing success.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Validation & Refinement">
    Run the track again. If your time improved but you feel the engine "bogging down" at the start, shorten the first gear slightly. Repeat until your acceleration is seamless.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Tuning Setting | Shorter Ratio Effect | Longer Ratio Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Acceleration** | **Increased** (Faster Launch) | Decreased (Slower Launch) |
| **Top Speed** | Decreased (Lower Ceiling) | **Increased** (Higher Ceiling) |
| **RPM Usage** | Reaches Redline Faster | Stays in Powerband Longer |
| **Best Use Case** | Short Drag Strips | Long Circuit Racing |
